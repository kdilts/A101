Simulated Photometry

Now we will use computer simulations to measure the light emitted by stars. In the process we will further explore the inverse square law for light. The interactive photometry exercise allows you to simulate measurements of the brightness of six different star. Each star has a different luminosity. Take about twenty measurements of each star. Make sure that you sample distances ranging from 15 to 150 (distance units are arbitrary in this case). When you are done with each star, the graph of the measured intensity (called flux in this case) as a function of distance should look pretty filled in.

Click here for an Interactive Photometry Exercise.

Please print out your results when you are done. You will need the printed page to answer some additional questions about the inverse square law of light.
